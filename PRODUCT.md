# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Editorial and product teams at Forbes.cz. They use these dashboards in the context of strategic work — reviewing membership performance, understanding content conversion patterns, and preparing for kick-off discussions. They are professionals who read data critically and don't need hand-holding.

## Product Purpose

A suite of internal strategy dashboards for Forbes.cz membership. Currently static kick-off documents; may evolve into live data views. The goal is to present analytical findings in a form that supports clear, fast decision-making — not to impress, but to inform.

## Brand Personality

Precise, editorial, unhurried.

The Forbes brand is present but quiet. These are working documents, not a showcase. The voice is confident and direct — no fluff, no over-explanation. Information is the product.

## Anti-references

- **Looker Studio / BI tools**: Cluttered chart grids, heavy chrome, color-coded everything, dashboard-for-the-sake-of-dashboard aesthetic.
- **Startup dashboards**: Gradient cards, hero metrics with big glowing numbers, performative data viz.
- **Notion-style docs**: Too much prose, not enough structure, no visual hierarchy.

## Design Principles

1. **Clarity is the feature.** Every visual choice must make the information faster to read. If it doesn't help comprehension, remove it.
2. **Editorial precision.** Typography and spacing carry the hierarchy. Chrome — borders, backgrounds, container chrome — stays quiet and earns no attention.
3. **Forbes brand, not Forbes template.** Apply the brand vocabulary (ForbesHighlander, sand/ink palette, thin borders) without reaching for clichés or decorative excess.
4. **Information density without clutter.** These readers are professionals. Respect their time with high signal-to-noise — no padding text, no redundant labels, no decorative whitespace.
5. **Built to grow.** Structure decisions as if live data will replace static values someday. Don't bake in layouts that only work for a fixed number of items.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG A as a minimum. Sufficient contrast on all text and data labels. Dark mode must meet the same bar as light mode. No information conveyed by color alone.
